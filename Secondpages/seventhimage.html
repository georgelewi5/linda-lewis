Family home, Ascot (Diploma Project)

The brief was to design a garden around a growing family who each needed their own outdoor space and to incorporate a home office and a productive area. The final design linked several ‘flexible’ spaces – a garden room (which could be a home office) led to a woodland outdoor area or ‘room’ (which could be used for eating or entertaining friends).

The long vista down the garden was mirrored in a productive garden with space for an orchard and vegetable/flower beds, all positioned around a large greenhouse (which could also be furnished as a conservatory or summer house) which, in addition to growing, could be used to entertain friends on a cool summer’s evening. The zones were partially divided by dry stone walls using local stone and planting was inspired by nearby woodland and Chobham Common.
